Calculator

Info

Designing a calculator web-based interaction with a focus on feedback (Feedback) and playfulness (Abstract Adjective) involves incorporating common nostalgic elements of the calculator experience with effective simulation of tactile elements and feedback through HTML, JavaScript and CSS to achieve a sense of play through actively engaging users. Actively engaging with the calculator - pressing buttons and manipulating the interface of the calculator fosters a sense of play and exploration. It can make the process of learning maths more enjoyable and interactive, leading to better engagement and understanding. The concept of play in relation to learning highlights how play allows individuals of all ages to engage in "natural experiments" and learn through exploration and experimentation (Gopnik, 2019).
Calculators have a nostalgic appeal that contributes to their playfulness. Many people have fond memories associated with calculators, whether from using them in school or playing with them as toys. The tactile nature of calculators plays a significant role in this nostalgia, making them playful and charming objects that spark joy and creativity. Simulating the tactile feedback of interacting with the physical object by button-pressing can be satisfying and playful, as it gives the user a sense of engagement and interaction. The act of pressing buttons and hearing the clicks can be enjoyable, reminiscent of playing with a mechanical toy. To simulate the feedback of button pressing, Skeuomorphism has been utilised as the design concept of making items represented resemble their real-world counterparts in how they appear and/or how the user can interact with them (The Interaction Design Foundation, 2015). This design style and effect has been achieved using CSS styling: Stacked radial and linear gradients, along with box shadows, have been employed to signify and encourage their affordance. By using :hover and :active pseudo-class styling, minor changes to scaling, gradients, and shadowing simulate the tactile sensation of pressing a physical button, providing satisfying and playful interactions.
The choice of a digital number font resembling a 7-segment LCD display enhances the playfulness of the design (Furr, 1997). This font style not only adds visual appeal but also evokes a sense of nostalgia for classic calculators, contributing to the overall playful experience. The calculator display being a shade of green is inspired by vacuum fluorescent displays commonly used in calculators. This choice not only pays homage to the traditional calculator design but also adds a nostalgic element to the user experience.
To further engage users and recreate the sensation of pressing physical buttons, a JavaScript audio function is called on button click. This function provides auditory feedback in the form of a key click sound, reminiscent of mechanical toy buttons. This auditory feedback adds another layer of delight and engagement to the calculator experience.
Immediate response plays a crucial role in creating a playful interaction. When users press a button on the calculator, the number or function should appear instantly on the display. This immediate feedback allows users to see the direct result of their input, reinforcing the cause-and-effect relationship between their actions and the calculator's response. The real-time outcomes of their inputs add an element of playfulness, enabling users to experiment and explore different possibilities.
In conclusion, designing a web-based calculator interaction with a focus on feedback and playfulness can create an engaging and enjoyable experience for users. By incorporating nostalgic elements, such as the tactile simulation of button pressing and the use of a digital number font resembling LCD displays, the calculator design evokes a sense of nostalgia and charm. The inclusion of auditory feedback through key click sounds further enhances the playful interaction. Additionally, the immediate response of the calculator to user inputs reinforces the cause-and-effect relationship, encouraging exploration and experimentation. Overall, these design choices and features contribute to an interactive and playful calculator experience that not only makes learning maths more enjoyable but also sparks creativity and engagement for users of all ages.
